Introduction

What are we testing and How do we think it will go?

Introduction

Hypothesis: We believe that using a smart lamp/ light bulb will be less time consuming and more accessible for young adults (from the ages of 19-26) than using a standard lamp/ light bulb.

Our teams assumptions are that by using the smart application a user will spend less time by having to complete fewer steps, than when using a standard lamp. The team also expects that as the user moves away from the lamp, using the application will become the quicker and convenient option.

Executive Summary (Barnum, 2010)

A usability testing team with members from Auckland University of Technology’s Human Computer Interaction paper - Tim Proctor, Violetta Oxenyuk, Adriaan Bosch and Wenjuan Zhang planned and conducted a usability test of the smart lamp at the HCI lab. The usability evaluation focus is 4.17.2 version Xiaomi Home - Xiaomi Smarthome App currently on the market. The hypothesis of the test is that by using a smart lamp/ light bulb will be less time consuming and more accessible for young adults (from the ages of 19-26) than using a standard lamp/ light bulb.

Testing with 6 participants took place on May 30. The findings meeting was held via Skype immediately after the last test. A spreadsheet of the findings was distributed to the team after the meeting as well as the user survey responses, which were recorded on google forms named ‘Survey Questions’ ("Survey Questions", 2019).

    The top findings are grouped into the following categories:

  • Aesthetics. Users commented that the interface can add some extra features.
  • Learnability. Most users found the instructions beginner-friendly.
  • Accessibility. Most users have encountered technical or connection problems which prevent them from controlling the smart lamp over app
